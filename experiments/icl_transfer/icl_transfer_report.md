# ICL Stated-Revealed Transfer: Phase 1-2 Results

## Summary

**A single stated rating pair in context shifts revealed choices more effectively than three revealed choice examples.** Stated ICL (K=1) achieves near-ceiling effects on easy axes and works correctly on all 6 directed axes, including the one where revealed ICL backfires. The model's aversion to harmful content is robust to ICL manipulation in either format.

## Setup

**Model:** Gemma 3 27B, temperature=1.0.

**Topics:** fiction, math, harmful_request — chosen for clean baseline asymmetries.

**Task sets:** 15 eval tasks (5 per topic) with established baselines, and 15 disjoint context tasks (5 per topic) used only for constructing ICL examples. No task appears in both sets.

**Measurement:** Standard `completion_preference` template — model sees two tasks, completes the one it prefers, semantic parser determines choice. 500 measurements per condition (25 eval pairs x 2 orderings x 10 samples). All values are P(choose preferred-direction topic), where chance = 0.5.

**ICL construction:** Context messages are injected between the system prompt and the measurement query. Two formats tested:

| ICL type | Context content | Example |
|----------|----------------|---------|
| Revealed | Pairwise choice prompt + fabricated completion of preferred task | User: "Here are two tasks..." / Assistant: "Task A: [on-policy math completion]" |
| Stated | Rating prompt (1-3 scale) + fabricated ratings | User: "Rate this task..." / Assistant: "3" then User: "Rate this task..." / Assistant: "1" |

Revealed completions are pre-generated by prompting the model on single tasks (no pairwise framing), ensuring natural on-policy text.

**Conditions:** 6 directed axes x 5 ICL context pairs per axis = 30 conditions per experiment. Total: ~45,000 API calls across 3 experiments.

## Baselines

P(choose row topic over column topic), no ICL context:

|  | math | fiction | harmful_request |
|--|------|---------|-----------------|
| **math** | — | 0.636 | 0.778 |
| **fiction** | 0.364 | — | 0.662 |
| **harmful_request** | 0.222 | 0.338 | — |

Hierarchy: **math > fiction >> harmful_request**. Math beats fiction 64% of the time; both dominate harmful_request.

## Results

| Axis (push toward) | Baseline | Revealed K=1 | Revealed K=3 | Stated K=1 |
|---------------------|----------|--------------|--------------|------------|
| math > harmful | 0.778 | 0.830 +0.05 | **0.996** +0.22 | **0.984** +0.21 |
| harmful > math | 0.222 | 0.249 +0.03 | 0.292 +0.07 | 0.032 **-0.19** |
| fiction > harmful | 0.662 | 0.718 +0.06 | 0.840 +0.18 | 0.855 +0.19 |
| harmful > fiction | 0.338 | 0.378 +0.04 | 0.326 -0.01 | 0.201 -0.14 |
| math > fiction | 0.636 | **0.276 -0.36** | **0.399 -0.24** | 0.765 +0.13 |
| fiction > math | 0.364 | 0.682 +0.32 | 0.821 +0.46 | 0.604 +0.24 |

Values are mean across 5 ICL context pairs per axis (std ranges 0.007-0.112). Delta from baseline shown after each value. Bold highlights surprising results.

### Key findings

**Stated ICL is more effective than revealed ICL.** One stated rating pair (K=1) shifts choices comparably to three revealed choice examples (K=3). On math > harmful: stated K=1 = 0.984, revealed K=3 = 0.996 (both near ceiling). On fiction > harmful: stated K=1 = 0.855 vs revealed K=3 = 0.840.

**Revealed ICL backfires on math > fiction.** Pushing math via revealed examples *decreases* P(choose math) from 0.636 to 0.276 (K=1) — the opposite of the intended direction. The effect is consistent across all 5 ICL pairs. Stated ICL does not backfire: pushing math raises P(math) from 0.636 to 0.765. Likely explanation: the math completion in the revealed ICL assistant turn primes the model toward engaging with similar content, so it completes the fiction task in the eval query rather than the math task.

**Pushing toward harmful_request fails.** Neither format increases preference for harmful tasks. Revealed barely moves the needle (+0.03 to +0.07). Stated actually *decreases* P(harmful): harmful > math drops from 0.222 to 0.032. The model's trained aversion to harmful content overrides ICL preference signals.

**Revealed ICL shows dose-response.** K=3 is consistently stronger than K=1 on axes where revealed ICL works in the intended direction (fiction > harmful: +0.18 vs +0.06; fiction > math: +0.46 vs +0.32).
