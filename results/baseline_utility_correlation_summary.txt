Baseline Utility Correlation Analysis
=====================================

This analysis computes the Pearson correlation (r) between baseline utilities and
condition utilities for OOD experiments 1b, 1c, and 1d. Results are reported both
with all tasks and excluding harmful tasks.

SUMMARY TABLE
=============

Experiment  | All Tasks           | Excluding Harmful
            | r ± SE              | r ± SE
------------|---------------------|------------------
exp1b       | 0.112 ± 0.039       | 0.112 ± 0.039
exp1c       | 0.589 ± 0.030       | 0.307 ± 0.043
exp1d       | 0.372 ± 0.046       | 0.122 ± 0.080

KEY FINDINGS
============

1. exp1b (hidden tasks):
   - Very low correlation with baseline (r ≈ 0.11), both with and without harmful tasks
   - This suggests system prompts have minimal effect on utility rankings
   - Note: exp1b has NO harmful tasks, so "all" and "excl harmful" are identical
   - High variability across conditions (range: -0.18 to 0.37)

2. exp1c (crossed tasks):
   - High correlation with baseline when including all tasks (r ≈ 0.59)
   - Drops substantially when excluding harmful tasks (r ≈ 0.31)
   - This indicates harmful tasks are driving much of the correlation
   - Suggests system prompts distort utility rankings, especially for harmful tasks
   - More consistent across conditions (lower SE)

3. exp1d (crossed tasks variant):
   - Moderate correlation with baseline overall (r ≈ 0.37)
   - Drops further when excluding harmful tasks (r ≈ 0.12)
   - Larger variance across conditions (SE ± 0.08 for no-harmful)
   - More heterogeneous effect of system prompts compared to exp1c

INTERPRETATION
==============

The pattern suggests:
- System prompts have their strongest effect on harmful task utilities
- In exp1c and exp1d, removing harmful tasks reveals much weaker baseline correspondence
- exp1b baseline concordance is weak across the board, suggesting a floor effect or
  genuine difficulty in ranking these OOD hidden tasks consistently

The large drop from "all tasks" to "excl harmful" in exp1c and exp1d indicates that
the system prompts are systematically reshuffling utility rankings, with the effect
being largest for harmful tasks.
